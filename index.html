Air-root-pruning mechanism

What the root animation shows

1
Pruning signal wave
After the first air-pruning point, a soft signal travels back along the main root. It represents loss of apical dominance and activation of lateral meristems higher on the root axis.
2
Activated lateral buds
Small translucent buds mark potential lateral initiation sites. Not every bud grows; selected buds mature, swell, and become new lateral roots.
3
Moisture + oxygen interface
The blue-green route marks the plastic-compost boundary, where moisture and oxygen are both available. Roots follow this interface rather than a purely geometric line.
4
Root tip at d=10 mm
At the outer cone tip, the root contacts air, loses turgor pressure, and the root cap is interrupted. The red pulse shows this short pruning stage.
5
Probabilistic cone selection
A lateral root may choose a nearby cone or a more distant active cone. The selected route reflects local microclimate and geometry, not a rigid nearest-point rule.

How the radar score is calculated

The figure presents the container as a coupled geometry-microclimate system. The aim is to compare tested radii by a combined score that rewards reliable root exit, strong lateral branching and fast air-pruning response.

J = wp·Pexit + wl·ρlat - wt·tdry
PexitRoot exit probability: the chance that the root tip reaches and exits through the air-pruning zone.
ρlatLateral branching density: the intensity of new lateral root formation after pruning.
tdryDrying time of the exposed root tip at the air-pruning point.
wp, wl, wtWeights that define the relative importance of each parameter in the optimisation model.
R = 76 mmBest overall balance: high exit probability, strong branching potential, fast drying and stable geometry-microclimate interaction.

Overlay radarThe area and shape of each profile show whether a configuration performs consistently, not only strongly on one isolated parameter.

The green R = 76 mm profile covers the largest and most balanced area on the radar chart. In practical terms, this means the geometry guides the root, the microclimate supports pruning, and the stress response stimulates lateral root formation as one integrated biophysical system.
